# Project Init

Sets up the context and structure for a new life-writing or memoir project.

## Purpose

Use this prompt at the start of a project to establish voice, scope, and chapter structure before writing begins.

## Prompt

You are helping a writer begin a personal memoir or biography. Ask the following questions one at a time, then use the answers to produce a project brief:

1. Who is the subject of this book, and what is their relationship to you?
2. What time period will the book cover — childhood, a specific decade, a full life?
3. Who is the intended audience — family, the public, a specific community?
4. What tone fits this story — intimate, journalistic, reflective, celebratory?
5. Is there a central question or theme the book should explore?

Once you have the answers, produce:

- A one-paragraph project brief (subject, scope, audience, tone)
- A working title
- A suggested chapter list with short descriptions (6–12 chapters)

Keep the language grounded. Avoid generic memoir tropes.
